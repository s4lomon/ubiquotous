Noninvasive neuroimaging enhances continuous neural tracking for robotic device control

B.J. Edelman, J. Meng, D. Suma, C. Zurn, E. Nagarajan, B.S. Baxter, C.C. Cline, B. He

This groundbreaking research demonstrates a systematic approach to achieving continuous robotic arm control through noninvasive brain-computer interfaces. The study achieved 60% improvement in BCI learning speed and 500% enhancement in continuous tracking ability using novel sensing and machine learning techniques with EEG source imaging.
