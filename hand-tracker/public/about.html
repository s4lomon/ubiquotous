Building the future of human-computer interaction

Building applications for brain-computer interface devices that make technology more intuitive, accessible, and aligned with human cognition.

What's being built

Brain-computer interfaces represent a fundamental shift in how humans interact with technology. Instead of adapting ourselves to the limitations of keyboards, mice, and touchscreens, we can create systems that understand and respond to our natural cognitive patterns.

Currently developing applications for existing BCI devices that focus on practical, everyday automation. The goal is to make technology that fades into the background—tools that work with your natural thought processes rather than interrupting them.

"The best interface is the one you don't notice. Building toward a future where interacting with technology is as natural as thinking."

Current focus

  • Task automation: Applications that let you control smartphones and computers through neural patterns, automating repetitive daily tasks without manual input.
  • Emotional intelligence: Systems that recognize mental states and emotional patterns, providing contextually relevant support and guidance.
  • AI integration: Combining BCI technology with large language models to create assistants that understand both what you're doing and how you're feeling, offering genuinely helpful decision support.
  • Accessibility: Making these tools available and useful for people with different abilities and needs, particularly those for whom traditional interfaces are challenging.

The path forward

This technology is still early. Today's BCI devices are like personal computers in the 1970s— functional but limited, promising but not yet fully realized. The work focuses on bridging that gap between what's technically possible and what's practically useful.

The applications being built today are steps toward more ambitious goals: systems that can predict your needs, interfaces that adapt to your cognitive state, and tools that genuinely augment human capability rather than just digitizing existing processes.
